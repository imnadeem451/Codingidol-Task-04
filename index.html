Maulana Abul Kalam Azad

About Legend


  • Born on November 11, 1888, in Mecca, in present day Saudi Arabia, to a family of Islamic scholars and theologians.
  • Named Abul Kalam Ghulam Mohiuddin Ahmed at birth.
  • His family later moved to Calcutta(Now Kolkata), where he spent hid formative years.
  • He recieved a traditional Islamic education from his father, learning languages, theology, and the Quran.
  • Despite his religious education, he also attended formal schools to learns sunject like mathematics and science.

  • Showed early signs of intellectual curiosity and social consiousness.
  • Became associated with the revolutionary and litrary circles in Calcutta.
  • Developed an interest in poetry, literature, and history.
  • Inspired bythe works of prominent Urdu and Persian poets.

  • Azad was drawn to the Indian National Congress's ideology of seeking freedom from British rule.
  • Participated actively in the Congress's activities during his youth.
  • Supported the Swadeshi Movement nad Non-Cooperation Movement led by Mahatma Gandhi in the 1920s.
  • Advocated for nonviolent resistance and civil disobedience as means of achieving independece.
  • Played a crucial role in forging Hindu-Muslim unity and communal harmony.

  • Recognized the significance of education in empowring thr masses.
  • Emphasized the importance of modern education alongside traditional learning.
  • Bielieved that education could bridge gaps and foster a sense of unity among diverse communities.
  • Helped establish the Jamia Millia Islamia University in Delhi in 1920, which aimed to provide both modern and religious educatiom.

  • Rose through the ranks of the Indian National Congress due to hid leadership qualities and commitment.
  • Became youngest president of the All India Congress Committee in 1923.
  • His speeches and writing emphasized the need for self-reliance,self-sufficiency, and the removal of social inequalities.

  • Faced imprisonment during the Civil Disobedience Movement for his participation in protests against the British government.
  • The struggle against colonial oppression took a toll on his health and well-being.
  • Despite hardship, he remained resolute in his commitment to the nation's cause.

  • Played a significant role in the post-independence era as a statesman and leader.
  • Served as the first Minister of Education in independent India under Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru.
  • Focused on shaping the education policies of the newly formed nation.
  • Advocated for free and compulsory education for all children up to the age of 14.

  • Azad was a local advocate of secularism and religious harmony.
  • Stressed the importance of equal treatment for all religious communities in a pluralistic society.
  • His leadership exemplified the values of unity in diversity and coexistence.

  • Maulana Abul Kalam Azad passed away on february 22, 1958, due to a stroke.
  • His death marked the end of a remarkable era of leadership and dedication to the nation's progress.
  • Azad's contributions to India's freedom struggle and his emphasis on education continue to inpire generations.
  • His legacy lives on through institutions like Jamia Millia Islamia and his litrary works, which provide valuable insights into India's history and struggle for independence.

Maulana Abul Kalam Azad's life journey encompassed a deep commitment to his nation, its people, and their aspirations for freedom and progress. From his early years in calcutta to his leadership roles in the Indian National Congress and independent India, Azad's influence resonates in his advocacy for education, religious harmony, and unity. His tireless effort continue to serve as a beacon of inspiration, reminding us of the power of determination, leadership, and the pursuit of a just and inclusive society.
